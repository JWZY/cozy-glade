## Description

A curious hybrid creature combining the stocky build of a wild boar with the powerful hind legs and bulbous eyes of a frog. Its mottled green-brown hide is speckled with darker spots, while a distinctive yellow underbelly marks it as a wetland dweller. Small tusks protrude from its snout, useful for rooting through mud and soil.

## Behavior

Frog Boars are **crepuscular foragers**, most active during dawn and dusk when the soil is damp and truffles release their strongest scent. Their powerful frog-like legs allow them to make surprising leaps when startled, despite their rotund appearance.

- **Diet**: Primarily truffles, mushrooms, roots, and aquatic plants
- **Social**: Found in small family groups called "puddles"
- **Mating**: Lays eggs in shallow water during spring rains
- **Lifespan**: 12-15 years

## Habitat

Found near **marshes, riverbanks, and flooded forests** where their amphibious nature gives them an advantage. They dig burrows in muddy banks that flood during high water, switching between air-breathing on land and absorbing oxygen through their skin underwater.
